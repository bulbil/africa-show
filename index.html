ESSAY ON AFRICA is an experiment in digital narrative. It exists as a complement to a longer, traditional piece of creative non-fiction. The piece leverages webcam data, flickr feeds, a poem by Laura Riding Jackson and a lot of JavaScript in an effort to get at what it means to recover the trace of ones coming to be.

In 1979 my father sold his home, quit his job and shipped a customized Dodge van to Rotterdam. My mother, four year old brother and he proceeded to drive for 13 months, visiting 38 countries across the length of Africa, ending in Capetown, South Africa.

Two months after they returned, I was born.

Recently, I found reels of slides along with a meticulous journal my father kept throughout their travels. The essay and this web complement are an effort to come to terms with just what these records might mean.
